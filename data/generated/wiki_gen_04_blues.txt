The blues is a kind of music. Like jazz, it started in the United States a long time ago. Black Americans made this music first. They made it from the songs they sang while they worked, and from sad songs about hard times. The blues grew in the southern part of the country, and then it spread to other places. Later, the blues helped to make many other kinds of music, like jazz and rock.

The blues got its name because it is often about sad feelings. When people say they feel blue, they mean they feel sad. Many blues songs are about hard times, lost love, hard work, or being far from home. But not all blues songs are sad. Some are about hope, and some are even happy. Even when the words are sad, the music can still feel good to hear.

The blues often uses a few simple parts that come back again and again. Many blues songs use the same kind of beat and the same way of building the song. Because of this, players who have never met can still play the blues together. One singer may sing a line, and then play a few notes on a guitar right after, almost like the guitar is answering the words.

The blues uses instruments like the guitar, the piano, and a small instrument that you blow into with your mouth. Often there is a singer who sings about his or her life and feelings. The voice in the blues is very important, because the blues is about telling how you feel.

The blues has had a very big effect on music all over the world. Many kinds of music that people love today came from the blues. Even now, many people still play the blues and listen to it, and it is still loved by people in many countries all over the world.
