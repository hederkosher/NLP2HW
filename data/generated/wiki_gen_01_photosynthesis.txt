Plants need food to live and grow, just like people and animals do. But plants do not eat food the way we eat. Plants make their own food. They make this food from sunlight, water, and air. The green parts of a plant, like the leaves, do most of this work. Inside the leaves there is a green stuff that can catch light from the sun. This green stuff is what makes leaves look green.

To make food, a plant needs three things. First, it needs light from the sun. Second, it needs water, which it pulls up from the ground through its roots. Third, it needs a gas from the air. The plant takes in this gas through tiny holes in its leaves. When the green stuff in the leaves catches the sunlight, the plant uses that light to join the water and the gas together. This makes a kind of sweet food that the plant can use to live and grow.

When the plant makes its food, it also makes a gas that goes back out into the air. This gas is the air that people and animals need to breathe. So plants help us in a very big way. Without plants making this gas, people and animals could not live.

Plants make the most food when there is a lot of light. On a bright, sunny day, the leaves work hard and make a lot of food. When it is dark at night, the plant cannot catch light, so it stops making food until the sun comes up again. The plant keeps some of its food to use right away, and it saves some of the food to use later.

This way of making food is very important for all life on Earth. Almost all living things need plants, either to eat the plants or to eat other animals that eat plants.
