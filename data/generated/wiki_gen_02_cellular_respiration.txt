All living things need energy to live. People, animals, and plants all need energy. They use energy to move, to grow, and to stay warm. Living things get this energy from food. But the energy in food is locked inside it. Living things must break the food down to let the energy out. This article is about how living things get energy from their food.

Inside every living thing there are very tiny parts called cells. Cells are so small that we cannot see them with just our eyes. A person is made of many, many cells. Each cell needs energy to do its work. To get energy, each cell takes in food and a gas from the air. This gas is the same gas that we breathe in when we take a breath.

Inside the cell, the food and the gas are joined together. When this happens, the food is broken down into smaller parts. As the food breaks down, the energy that was locked inside it comes out. The cell can then use this energy to do its work. This is how a cell turns food into energy it can use.

When the cell breaks down the food, it also makes some waste. One waste is a gas, and one waste is water. The body must get rid of this waste gas. We push it out of our body each time we breathe out. So when we breathe in, we take in the good gas, and when we breathe out, we push out the waste gas.

This way of getting energy from food never stops. It goes on all day and all night, in every cell, for as long as the living thing is alive. Without it, no living thing could move or grow or stay alive. This is why we must keep eating food and keep breathing air every day of our lives.
